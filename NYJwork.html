Born A Crime

Trevor Noah wrote a book titled Born a Crime. He was born in a time when black South Africans were divided into tribes during colonialism, before apartheid divided the nation's people according to race. The two main groupings were the warrior Zulu people and the Xhosa people, who utilized tactics to repel the European invasion. This tactic resulted in prejudice and a lack of freedom, whereby one's ability to go around was restricted due to one's race. Noah considers the complexity of race, identity, family, and religion that the marginalized communities in K.C. experience in today's society.
